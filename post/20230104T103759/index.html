[Calculus] 1. Dedekind Cut

2023-01-04
#calculus #math

Definition 1.1. Let \(S\sube\R\) and \(r\in\R\). We say that:

  1. \(r\) is an upper(lower) bound of \(S\) if $∀ s∈ S[r≥(≠)s]
  2. \(r\) is the greatest(least) element of \(S\) if:
    1. \(r\) is an upper(lower) bound of \(s\)
    2. \(r\in S\)
  3. \(r\) is the least upper(greatest lower) bound of \(S\) if \(r=\min\{u\in\R|u\text{ is an upper(lower) bound of }S\}\). In this case, \(r\) is also denoted as \(\sup S(\inf S)\).

Definition 1.2 (Dedekind cut). Let \(A,B\sube\R\). We say that \((A,B)\) is a Dedekind cut of $\R\ if:

  1. \(A\ne B\ne\varnothing\)
  2. \(A\cup B=\R\)
  3. \(\forall a\in A,b\in B[a < b]\)

Proposition 1.3 (Dedekind's gapless property). If \((A,B)\) is a Dedekind cut of \(\R\), then exactly one of the following happens:

  • \(\max A\) exists, but \(\min B\) doesn't
  • \(\min B\) exists, but \(\max A\) doesn't

Theorem 1.4 (Weierstrass). Let \(S(\ne\varnothing)\sube\R\). If S has an upper bound, then \(\sup S\) exists.

Proof. Let \(B:=\{b\in\R|b\text{ is an upper(lower) bound of }S\}\), and \(A:=\R\backslash B\).

Since:

  1. \(S\ne\varnothing\Rarr A\ne\varnothing\), and '\(S\) has an upper bound' \(\rArr B\ne\varnothing\).
  2. \(A\cup B\) by definition.
  3. \(\forall a\in A,b\in B[a < b]\). Were this false, \(a\ge b\Rarr a\text{ is an upper bound of }S\Rarr a\in B\), a contradiction.

Therefore, \((A,B)\) is a Dedekind cut.

Suppose \(\max A\) exists, denoted as \(a_0\). Then \(a_0\in A\Rarr a_0\notin B\Rarr a_0\text{ is not an upper bound of }S \Rarr \exists s_0 \in S [a_0 < s_0] \Rarr s_0 \notin A \Rarr s_0 \in B \Rarr S_0 \text{ is an upper bound of } S\), a contradiction. i.e., \(\max A\) doesn't exists.

By Dedekind's gapless property, \(\min B\) must exist, i.e., \(\sup S\) exists. □
